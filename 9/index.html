Question:

Prove that if \(a\) and \(b\) are two relatively prime integers, and we have some integer \(c\), such that \(c \mid a\), then \(c\) and \(b\) are relatively prime.

Toggle answer

Solution:

Let \(d = \gcd(c, b)\). Then $$ d \mid b \text{ and } d \mid c. $$ Given that \(c \mid a\), we have that $$ d \mid c \text{ and } c \mid a \implies d \mid a $$ by the transitivity property of divisibility. This means that \(d\) is a common divisor of \(a\) and \(b\). Since every common divisor divides the gcd, \(d \mid \gcd(a, b) = 1\), it must be that \(d = 1\).

This proves that \(\gcd(c, b) = 1\). That is, \(c\) and \(b\) are relatively prime.
